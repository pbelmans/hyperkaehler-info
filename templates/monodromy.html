Monodromy group

The monodromy group is generated by parallel transport operators in smooth families of hyperk√§hler manifolds containing $X$ as a fiber. One distinguishes the full cohomological monodromy \[ \operatorname{Mon}(X)\subseteq\operatorname{GL}(\operatorname{H}^\ast(X,\mathbb{Z})) \] and the degree-two monodromy \[ \operatorname{Mon}^2(X)\subseteq\operatorname{O}(\operatorname{H}^2(X,\mathbb{Z}),\mathrm{q}_X), \] where the Beauville–Bogomolov form $\mathrm{q}_X$ depends only on the deformation type. By definition, monodromy is a deformation invariant.

The discriminant group of an integral lattice $L$ is \[ \operatorname{A}_L := L^\vee\!/L, \] where $L^\vee=\operatorname{Hom}(L,\mathbb{Z})$ is the dual lattice. It is a finite abelian group whose order equals $|\det\operatorname{Gram}(L)|$. For $L=\operatorname{H}^2(X,\mathbb{Z})$ equipped with $\mathrm{q}_X$, the discriminant group $\operatorname{A}_{\operatorname{H}^2(X,\mathbb{Z})}$ is the group listed in the BBF table (e.g.\ $\mathbb{Z}/(2n-2)\mathbb{Z}$ for $\mathrm{K3}^{[n]}$-type).

Let \[ \mathrm{W}_X:=\{f\in \operatorname{O}^{+}(\operatorname{H}^2(X,\mathbb{Z}),\mathrm{q}_X)\mid f\text{ acts on }\operatorname{A}_{\operatorname{H}^2(X,\mathbb{Z})}\text{ as }\pm\operatorname{id}\}, \] an index-$2^a$ subgroup of $\operatorname{O}^{+}$. For $\operatorname{Kum}^{n}$-type, write $\chi$ for the character induced by the discriminant action.
