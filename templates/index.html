Varieties with trivial canonical bundle are particularly interesting objects. The Beauville–Bogomolov decomposition theorem tells us that, after a finite étale cover, such a variety is isomorphic to the product of

  • abelian varieties
  • strict Calabi–Yau varieties
  • irreducible holomorphic symplectic, or hyperkähler varieties

The classification for the first class is trivial, whilst there are many constructions for the second class, already in dimension 3.

The classification of the third class is the most intriguing: hyperkähler varieties have lots of amazing properties, but there are very few examples. We collect (some) information about the known families.
