Euler characteristic

The topological Euler characteristic is the alternating sum of the Betti numbers of a manifold. It is also the top Chern number, so for a $2n$-dimensional hyperk√§hler manifold $X$ we are interested in $\int\mathrm{c}_{2n}(X)$.

For the two infinite families we obtain an important sequence of numbers, as detailed below.

1, 24, 324, 3200, 25650, 176256, 1073720, 5930496, 30178575, 143184000, 639249300, ...
These are the coefficients of the expansion of $1/\eta(q)^{24}$, see also OEIS:A006922.

These are the coefficients of the expansion of $\frac{1}{6912}(6\mathrm{E}_2^2\mathrm{E}_4 - 8\mathrm{E}_2\mathrm{E}_6 + 3\mathrm{E}_4^2 - \mathrm{E}_2^4)$, where $\mathrm{E}_2,\mathrm{E}_4,\mathrm{E}_6$ are the Eisenstein series of weights 2, 4, and 6, see also OEIS:A282211.
